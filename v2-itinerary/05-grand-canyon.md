## Highlights

### Mather Point
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mather+Point+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/mather-point.htm)
- THE iconic first view of the Grand Canyon
- Large viewing area with railings
- Near Visitor Center
- Sunrise and sunset popular

> **Kid-Friendly:** Paved, fenced viewing area. Safe for kids. The view will leave everyone speechless!

---

### Rim Trail
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rim+Trail+Grand+Canyon+South+Rim+AZ)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/day-hiking.htm)
- 21 km total along the rim (do sections!)
- Paved between Mather Point and Bright Angel Trailhead
- Mostly flat, stroller-friendly on paved sections
- Continuous canyon views

> **Kid-Friendly:** The paved section from the Village to Yavapai Point (~2 km) is perfect for families. Stroller-friendly, fenced in spots, and stunning views the whole way!

**Recommended Rim Trail Sections:**

| Section | Distance | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Mather Point to Yavapai Point | 1.2 km | Paved, stroller-friendly |
| Village to Bright Angel Trailhead | 1 km | Paved, easy |
| Yavapai Point to Village | 1 km | Paved |

---

### Yavapai Geology Museum
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Yavapai+Geology+Museum+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/museum-collection-yavapai.htm)
- Window wall overlooking the canyon
- Interactive geology exhibits
- Learn how the canyon formed
- **Free** with park entry

> **Kid-Friendly:** Great for curious kids! The 5-year-old will love learning how the canyon was made.

---

### Desert View Watchtower
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Desert+View+Watchtower+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/desert-view.htm)
- 21m stone tower designed by Mary Colter
- Climb spiral stairs for panoramic views
- Gift shop and restrooms
- 40 km east of Village on Desert View Drive

> **Kid-Friendly:** The 5-year-old can climb the tower (narrow stairs, manageable). Beautiful murals inside. The 18-month-old may need to be carried.

---

### Grand Canyon IMAX
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Grand+Canyon+IMAX+Theater+Tusayan+AZ)
- [Official Website](https://www.explorethecanyon.com)
- 34-minute movie on giant screen
- "Grand Canyon: Rivers of Time"
- Located in Tusayan (just outside park)
- Air-conditioned break from the heat/cold!
- **Cost:** ~$15 adults, $12 children

> **Kid-Friendly:** Great activity for rest of the feet! Stunning footage, interesting for all ages.

---

### Bright Angel Trail (Short Walk)
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bright+Angel+Trailhead+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/bright-angel-trail.htm)
- Most popular trail INTO the canyon
- Just walk to the first tunnel (300m) for a taste
- **DO NOT** attempt a full hike with young children!
- Steep and strenuous

> **Kid-Friendly Caution:** A very short walk (first few hundred meters only!) gives kids the experience of going into the canyon. The trail is steep - hold hands, use carrier for 18-month-old. Turn back early!

---

### Hermit Road Viewpoints
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hermits+Rest+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/hermit-road.htm)
- 11 km scenic road west of Village
- Shuttle-only March-November (no private cars)
- 9 viewpoints along the way
- Hopi Point is great for sunset

> **Kid-Friendly:** Free shuttle, hop on/off. Good if you have extra time.

## Desert View Drive

A must-do scenic drive along the eastern South Rim:

| Stop | Distance from Village | Highlights |
|------|----------------------|------------|
| Yaki Point | 3 km | Great sunrise spot |
| Grandview Point | 19 km | One of the highest viewpoints |
| Moran Point | 26 km | Named after landscape painter |
| Tusayan Ruin | 35 km | 800-year-old Puebloan ruins |
| Desert View | 40 km | Watchtower, panoramic views |

**Tip:** This drive is in your own car (not shuttle-only). Plan 2-3 hours for the round trip with stops.

## Practical Information

### Getting Around

**Free Shuttles:**
- [NPS Shuttle Info](https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/shuttle-buses.htm)
- Village Route: Around Grand Canyon Village
- Kaibab Route: Visitor Center to Yaki Point
- Hermit Route: Along Hermit Road (seasonal)
- All free, run frequently

**Parking:**
- Park once at the main lots near Visitor Center
- Use shuttles to explore
- Lots fill by 10 AM in busy season

### Where to Eat

**Yavapai Lodge Restaurant**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Yavapai+Lodge+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- Cafeteria style, casual
- Burgers, pizza, salads
- Kid-friendly

**Bright Angel Restaurant**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bright+Angel+Lodge+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- [Official Website](https://www.grandcanyonlodges.com/dining/bright-angel-dining/)
- Full service restaurant
- Western-themed
- Reservations helpful for dinner

**El Tovar Dining Room**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=El+Tovar+Hotel+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- [Official Website](https://www.grandcanyonlodges.com/dining/el-tovar-dining-room/)
- Upscale option, historic hotel
- Reservations required
- Dress code (smart casual)

**Market Plaza Deli**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Market+Plaza+Grand+Canyon+AZ)
- Quick sandwiches, groceries
- Good for picnic supplies

**Tusayan (outside park):**
- Pizza Hut, McDonald's, and several family restaurants
- We Cookk - good BBQ
- Canyon Star Restaurant (steakhouse)

### Baby Facilities
- Visitor Center has changing facilities
- All lodge restaurants are family-friendly
- General Store has baby supplies

### Weather & What to Pack

**Late April/Early May Weather:**
- Daytime: 15-22°C (pleasant)
- Morning/Evening: 5-10°C (COLD!)
- Rim is at 2,100m - cooler than you expect
- Wind can be strong

**Pack:**
- Warm layers (it's colder than the desert below!)
- Jacket for morning/evening
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sunscreen, sunglasses
- Water bottles
- Camera!

### Wildlife
- Elk very common (especially morning/evening)
- California condors sometimes spotted soaring
- Rock squirrels (don't feed!)
- Ravens everywhere

## Optional: Horseshoe Bend (En Route)

If driving from Zion via Page:

**Horseshoe Bend**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Horseshoe+Bend+Page+AZ)
- [Official Website](https://www.visitarizona.com/directory/horseshoe-bend/)
- Iconic Colorado River horseshoe
- 2.4 km round trip walk
- NO shade, can be hot
- **Cost:** $10 parking

> **Kid-Friendly Caution:** Beautiful but NO railings at the edge. Hold kids' hands firmly! The 18-month-old should stay in carrier. If nervous about drop-offs, skip it.
