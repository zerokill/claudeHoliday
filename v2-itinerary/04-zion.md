## Highlights

### Riverside Walk
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Riverside+Walk+Trailhead+Zion+National+Park+UT)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/thenarrows.htm)
- 3.2 km round trip, paved the entire way
- Ends at the mouth of The Narrows
- Towering canyon walls on both sides
- Kids can wade in the Virgin River at the end
- Shuttle stop: Temple of Sinawava (Stop 9)

> **Kid-Friendly:** THE best trail in Zion for families! Completely paved, stroller-friendly, and the river at the end is magical. Bring water shoes for wading!

---

### Pa'rus Trail
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Parus+Trail+Zion+National+Park+UT)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/parus-trail.htm)
- 5.6 km one-way (or do partial)
- Flat, paved, stroller and bike friendly
- Along the Virgin River
- No shuttle needed - starts near Visitor Center
- Mountain and canyon views

> **Kid-Friendly:** Perfect for strollers and tired legs. Flat, easy, scenic. Good for arrival afternoon.

---

### Lower Emerald Pools Trail
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Emerald+Pools+Trail+Zion+National+Park+UT)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/emerald-pools-702702.htm)
- 1.9 km round trip to Lower Pool
- Paved but some mild elevation gain
- Small waterfall (seasonal)
- Shuttle stop: Zion Lodge (Stop 5)

> **Kid-Friendly:** The 5-year-old can walk this. Stroller possible but bumpy in spots. Upper/Middle pools are steeper - stick to Lower with young kids.

---

### Weeping Rock
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Weeping+Rock+Zion+National+Park+UT)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/weeping-rock.htm)
- 0.7 km round trip
- Short, steep paved path
- Water seeping from rock overhang
- Shuttle stop: Weeping Rock (Stop 7)

> **Kid-Friendly:** Very short! The dripping water from the rock is fascinating for kids.

---

### Canyon Overlook Trail
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Canyon+Overlook+Trail+Zion+National+Park+UT)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/canyon-overlook-trail.htm)
- 1.6 km round trip
- Some uneven terrain and drop-offs (use caution!)
- Spectacular views of Zion Canyon
- Parking at east tunnel entrance (limited!)

> **Kid-Friendly Caution:** Great for families with confident walkers. The 5-year-old can do it with supervision. NOT stroller-friendly. Keep kids close to avoid drop-offs.

---

### Zion Canyon Scenic Drive
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zion+Canyon+Scenic+Drive+UT)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/zion-canyon-scenic-drive.htm)
- 10 km one-way into the canyon
- **Shuttle only** (April-October) - no private cars
- 9 stops, hop on/off all day
- Views of Court of the Patriarchs, Zion Lodge, The Narrows entrance

> **Kid-Friendly:** The shuttle is easy with kids and strollers. Get on, get off, explore, repeat!

---

### Zion-Mt. Carmel Highway
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zion+Mount+Carmel+Highway+UT)
- [NPS Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/driving-in-zion.htm)
- Scenic drive from Springdale to east entrance
- Historic 1.8 km tunnel (one-way traffic for large vehicles)
- Switchbacks with canyon views
- Checkerboard Mesa

> **Kid-Friendly:** A beautiful drive! Stop at pullouts for photos. The tunnel is exciting for kids.

## The Shuttle System

### How It Works
- [NPS Shuttle Info](https://www.nps.gov/zion/planyourvisit/zion-canyon-shuttle-system.htm)
- **Mandatory** April-October (no private cars in canyon)
- **Free** with park entry
- Runs every 7-10 minutes
- Stroller-friendly (fold up when crowded)
- First shuttle: ~6 AM, Last return: ~9 PM

### Tips
- Start early (before 9 AM) to avoid crowds
- Bring snacks and water on the shuttle
- The last stop (Temple of Sinawava) is most popular - go first thing
- Shuttles pick up in Springdale too (free town shuttle)

## Practical Information

### Getting Around

**Shuttle:**
- Park shuttle is free and mandatory for Zion Canyon
- Town shuttle also free - runs through Springdale to Visitor Center
- Leave your car at hotel and shuttle everywhere!

**Parking:**
- Limited at Visitor Center (fills by 8-9 AM)
- Park at hotel and use town shuttle
- Free parking in Springdale with shuttle access

### Where to Eat

**Oscar's Cafe**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Oscars+Cafe+Springdale+UT)
- [Official Website](https://oscarscafe.com)
- Great Mexican food
- Outdoor seating with views
- Kid-friendly

**Cafe Soleil**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cafe+Soleil+Springdale+UT)
- Breakfast and coffee
- Outdoor patio

**Whiptail Grill**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Whiptail+Grill+Springdale+UT)
- Casual, good for families
- Mexican-American food

**Zion Lodge Red Rock Grill**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zion+Lodge+Utah)
- [Official Website](https://www.zionlodge.com/dining/)
- Inside the park (shuttle stop 5)
- Full service restaurant
- Good for lunch during park day

**Tip:** Make dinner reservations or go early - Springdale restaurants fill up!

### Baby Facilities
- Visitor Center has changing facilities
- Zion Lodge has family restrooms
- Most Springdale restaurants are family-friendly

### Weather & What to Pack

**Late April Weather:**
- Daytime: 20-25°C (very pleasant!)
- Morning/Evening: 10-15°C (cool)
- Afternoon sun can be strong
- Rain possible but unlikely

**Pack:**
- Water shoes (for river wading) - essential!
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Layers
- Sunscreen, hats
- Water bottles
- Picnic supplies

### Wildlife
- Mule deer common
- Rock squirrels (don't feed!)
- California condors occasionally spotted
- Bighorn sheep on cliffs (rare)
