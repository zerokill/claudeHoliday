## Highlights

### Mono Lake
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mono+Lake+South+Tufa+CA)
- [Official Website](https://www.monolake.org)
- Ancient salt lake with bizarre tufa towers
- 2-3 million years old!
- Millions of migratory birds
- South Tufa area has the best formations
- **Cost:** $3/person (cash, honor system)

**South Tufa Trail:**
- 1.6 km easy loop walk
- Flat, mostly sandy
- Stroller possible but sandy in spots
- Amazing photo opportunities

> **Kid-Friendly:** The tufa towers look like alien landscapes! Kids love exploring around them. The walk is easy. Look for brine shrimp in the salty water!

---

### Hot Creek Geological Site
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hot+Creek+Geological+Site+Mammoth+Lakes+CA)
- [Official Website](https://www.fs.usda.gov/recarea/inyo/recarea/?recid=20254)
- Active geothermal area
- Steaming hot springs in a creek
- Boardwalk viewing area
- **NO SWIMMING** - water is dangerously hot!
- **FREE**

> **Kid-Friendly:** Fascinating to see! Steam rising from the ground and water. Keep kids on the boardwalk - the water is scalding hot in places. Great for explaining geology!

---

### Alabama Hills
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Alabama+Hills+Lone+Pine+CA)
- [Official Website](https://www.blm.gov/visit/alabama-hills)
- Rounded granite boulders with Sierra Nevada backdrop
- Used in hundreds of movies and TV shows (Lone Ranger, Iron Man, etc.)
- Movie Road scenic drive
- Natural arches and rock formations
- **FREE**

**Mobius Arch:**
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mobius+Arch+Alabama+Hills+CA)
- Short walk (~0.5 km) to natural arch
- Mt. Whitney framed in the arch!
- Easy for families

> **Kid-Friendly:** Kids love climbing on the boulders! It's like a natural playground. Bring a camera - the views of Mt. Whitney are incredible.

---

### June Lake Loop (Scenic Drive)
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=June+Lake+Loop+CA)
- 26 km scenic loop off US-395
- Four beautiful alpine lakes
- Mountain views
- Good for a picnic stop

> **Kid-Friendly:** Pretty drive with lake views. Stop at one of the lakes for a leg stretch.

---

### Whoa Nellie Deli
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Whoa+Nellie+Deli+Lee+Vining+CA)
- [Official Website](https://www.whoanelliedeli.com)
- Famous gas station deli with gourmet food!
- Fish tacos, lobster taquitos, burgers
- At the Mobil gas station in Lee Vining
- Outdoor seating with Mono Lake views
- **Seasonal** - check if open in early May

> **Kid-Friendly:** It's a gas station... with amazing food! Kids will think it's funny. Great burgers and outdoor seating.

### Weather & What to Pack

**Early May Weather:**
- Mornings: 5-10°C (COLD at elevation!)
- Afternoons: 15-20°C (pleasant)
- Can be windy
- Higher elevations are cooler

**Pack:**
- Warm layers (it's colder than you expect!)
- Jacket for mornings
- Comfortable shoes for walking on uneven ground
- Sunscreen, sunglasses
- Camera!

### Wildlife
- Mule deer common
- Rabbits, ground squirrels
- Various birds at Mono Lake

## Important: Tioga Pass Status

If driving to San Francisco via Yosemite, you need Tioga Pass (Highway 120) to be open.

**Tioga Pass typically opens:** Late May to early June
**In early May:** It will likely still be CLOSED!

**Check status:** [Caltrans Road Information](https://roads.dot.ca.gov/)

**If Tioga is closed, your route options:**
1. **Via Sonora Pass (Highway 108):** Often opens earlier than Tioga, scenic
2. **Via Reno:** US-395 North to Reno → I-80 West to SF (longer but reliable)

**Columbia State Historic Park** is accessible via the Sonora route!

**Drive:** 5-6 hours (~450 km)
**Depart:** Morning of May 5

**Columbia State Historic Park** (if going via Sonora):
- [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Columbia+State+Historic+Park+CA)
- [Official Website](https://www.parks.ca.gov/?page_id=552)
- Preserved 1850s gold rush town
- Stagecoach rides, gold panning, candy shops
- FREE to walk around (activities cost extra)
- Perfect stop en route to SF!

> **Kid-Friendly:** Like stepping back in time! Gold panning is a huge hit with kids. Historic buildings, blacksmith, stagecoach rides. Plan 1-2 hours.
